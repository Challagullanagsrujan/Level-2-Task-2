About Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar is a former Indian cricketer and one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. He was born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India.

Tendulkar made his debut for the Indian national team at the age of 16 and went on to have an illustrious career that spanned over two decades. He is the leading run-scorer in both Test and ODI cricket and has numerous records to his name.

Career

Tendulkar played his first Test match against Pakistan in 1989 and his first ODI match against the same opposition in 1989. He went on to play 200 Tests and 463 ODIs for India, scoring a total of 34,357 runs across both formats.

Some of Tendulkar's most memorable innings include his century on debut in the Ranji Trophy, his 'Desert Storm' innings in Sharjah, and his double century against South Africa in 2010.

Achievements

  • Leading run-scorer in Test cricket with 15,921 runs
  • Leading run-scorer in ODI cricket with 18,426 runs
  • Only player to score 100 international centuries
  • Won the World Cup with India in 2011
